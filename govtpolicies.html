Stubble Burning: An Environmental Crisis

The Hindu: Delhi is blanketed in smog. AQI rising rapidly! Citizens advised to follow strict rules. Fine imposed on rule-breakers.

Stubble burning is a significant issue in India, primarily due to the widespread practice of burning crop residues post-harvest. This practice contributes to severe air pollution, releases harmful greenhouse gases, and degrades soil quality. It poses significant health risks to both rural and urban populations and negatively impacts biodiversity and overall ecosystem health. The need for an effective, sustainable solution is urgent.

Government Challenges

Incapabilities and Shortcomes

Tribune India: Are Reorient government policies going to put an ultimate end stubble burning?

Despite recognizing the gravity of the problem, the government has faced several challenges in effectively addressing stubble burning. The reach of subsidies and incentives has been limited, resulting in uneven and delayed distribution of benefits. Awareness campaigns aimed at educating farmers about the harmful effects of stubble burning have often lacked the necessary reach and impact. Language barriers, inadequate resources, and poor execution have hindered the effectiveness of these initiatives.
